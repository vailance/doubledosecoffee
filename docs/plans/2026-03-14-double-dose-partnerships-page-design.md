# Double Dose Coffee Partnerships Page Design

**Goal:** Rebuild the Partnerships page as a retail-first B2B page that still supports secondary wholesale and collaboration inquiries.

## Direction

The current page is too broad and generic to feel credible. The approved direction is a retail-first partnerships page: one that clearly leads with stockist and retail opportunities while still leaving room for cafes, restaurants, and brand collaborations as secondary paths.

The page should feel selective, confident, and polished rather than overly corporate or aggressively sales-driven.

## Page Structure

### Hero

- Replace the generic "Collaborate / Partnerships" framing with a more specific retail-led message.
- Keep the page visually aligned with the rest of the site.

### Primary Retail Section

- Explain who the page is for and why Double Dose fits selected retail spaces.
- Make retail and stockist conversations the clear main path.

### Secondary Partnership Paths

- Introduce wholesale supply and collaborations as valid but secondary routes.
- Make the difference between the three paths easy to understand.

### What Partners Can Expect

- Present a short benefits or process section that makes the page feel more concrete and useful.
- Keep claims specific enough to sound intentional without inventing unsupported operational detail.

### Closing CTA

- Replace the vague contact CTA with a clearer partnership inquiry invitation.

## Visual Direction

- Reuse the established cinematic editorial language from the other upgraded pages.
- Use stronger hierarchy, layered surfaces, and warmer B2B styling.
- Keep the page easy to scan despite the more brand-led feel.

## Accessibility and Motion

- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Keep CTA contrast and focus states strong.
- Preserve clear reading order and distinction between the three partnership types.

## Files Expected To Change

- `src/app/partnerships/page.tsx`
- `src/components/partnerships/*`
- `docs/plans/*`

## Notes

The page should not invent logistics, MOQs, or wholesale terms that were not provided. It should stay persuasive while avoiding false specificity.
